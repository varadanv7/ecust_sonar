How can I identify which toll roads are managed by Fort Bend County?
Fort Bend County manages the Fort Bend Grand Parkway (SH-99) from US-59 to Fry Rd, Fort Bend Parkway from Beltway 8 to Sienna Parkway and the Westpark Tollway from SH-99, the Grand Parkway, to FM 1464 in Houston. You may also refer to the Fort Bend Toll Roads Map link on our homepage.
I have a Tag account, why did it not work on the Fort Bend County Toll Roads?
TxTAG (Texas Tollways), EZ Tag (Harris County Toll Road Authority), or TollTag (North Texas Tollway Authority) account holders may also receive Notices/Violations. The most frequent reasons as to why the notice was received could be due to the following:
  • The toll tag is not properly mounted
  • The credit card data on file for the toll tag account has expired
  • You have a new car/new license plate and your account was not properly updated
  • There are insufficient funds on the account
  • The toll tag is defective.

Under what conditions can I get a violation?
The Fort Bend County Toll Road Authority electronic toll collection system operates toll lanes exclusively for authorized TxTAG (Texas Tollways), EZ Tag (Harris County Toll Road Authority), or Toll Tag (North Texas Tollway Authority) tag holders. Non tag holders that use the dedicated toll lanes incur violations and have images of their license plates recorded by a video system. In the event you have a tag account with one of the before mentioned agencies, you may have received the toll violation notice if your tag account was not valid at the time of the transaction.
I have a valid tag account and I sometimes pull a camper or trailer with my vehicle. Can I still use a Fort Bend County toll road?
Yes, the toll rate will automatically calculate based on the amount of axles on your vehicle and trailer. You may also refer to the Fort Bend Toll Roads Map link on our homepage for toll rates by plaza.
Do I need a Tag account to travel on a Fort Bend County toll road?
Yes, the Fort Bend County Toll Road Authority electronic toll collection system operates toll lanes exclusively for authorized tag account holders.

I received a notice/violation that I would like to dispute. What should I do?
For a Sold/Lease or Rented vehicle, please attach copy of proof of sale, copy of lease or rental agreement with the notice coupon and mail to Fort Bend County Toll Road Authority, 16107 Kensington Drive, #1008, Sugar Land, TX 77479.

For Stolen, please attach copy of police report or copy of insurance claim with the notice coupon and mail to Fort Bend County Toll Road Authority, 16107 Kensington Drive, #1008, Sugar Land, TX 77479.

For valid tag account holders, please include toll payment and provide proof of a valid tag account (Vehicle Tag List/Account History) to Fort Bend County Toll Road Authority, 16107 Kensington Drive, #1008, Sugar Land, TX 77479.

How can I check the status of a Notice/Violation?
Status of a notice can be found on our website. You will need your violation notice number, license plate and license plate state to check the status.
What is a Determination Letter?
Upon receipt of dispute and support documentation, a determination is made by the FBTRCSC and a Determination Letter is generated which will advise the status of a toll violation dispute.

I have an active Tag account, why do I see different toll rates on my account statement?
Fort Bend County has Valid Tag and Invalid/Fleet toll rates. If a valid tag is read in the lane at the time of transaction, the Valid Tag rate is applied. If a tag is not present in the vehicle or an invalid transaction occurs, the Invalid/Fleet toll rate is applied.
