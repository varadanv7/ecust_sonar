THE FASTRAK FLEX TOLL TAG
The FasTrak Flex toll tag works like a standard FasTrak toll tag and has an additional feature required for carpools in the I-580 Express Lanes.
The FasTrak Flex toll tag works as a standard FasTrak toll tag but also has a three-position switch to signal whether you are travelling as a carpool or alone. It is the carpool toll tag for all Bay Area Express Lanes.
The standard FasTrak toll tag works at all Bay Area toll bridges, in express lanes for solo drivers, and pays SFO parking.
Should You Get a FasTrak Flex Toll Tag?
Yes, for carpool travel in the I-580 Express Lanes.
Get the FasTrak Flex toll tag if you want to travel toll-free as a carpool, motorcycle, or eligible Clean Air Vehicle (CAV) in the new I-580 Express Lanes. The FasTrak Flex toll tag also works as a standard FasTrak toll tag. It pays bridge tolls, express lanes as a solo driver, and parking at garages displaying the FasTrak logo.
Yes, if you want more convenient travel as a carpool in other express lanes.
The FasTrak Flex toll tag is a convenience, but is not required, for toll-free travel in the SR-237 and I-680 Express Lanes as a carpool, motorcycle, or eligible CAV. If you have a standard FasTrak toll tag, you must enclose it in a Mylar bag to avoid being charged a toll. If you have a FasTrak Flex toll tag, you can leave it mounted on your windshield and use the switch position to identify your vehicle for toll-free travel.
You can carpool toll-free in the midday, too
It takes only two people to be a carpool and travel toll-free, any time between 5 am to 8 pm, Monday through Friday including holidays in the I-580 Express Lanes. A FasTrak Flex toll tag is required, so get yours today.
TIP
Otherwise, no.
The standard FasTrak toll tag will serve all your needs if you do not use express lanes as a carpool. There is no need to obtain a FasTrak Flex toll tag. The vast majority of FasTrak users do not need a FasTrak Flex toll tag.
