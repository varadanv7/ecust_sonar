Commute Bus or Vanpool Vehicle

A Commute Bus or Vanpool Vehicle may cross toll-free at any time in designated lane(s), in accordance with operational procedures.

Passage through staffed lanes requires a toll-free commute bus ticket or a non-revenue FasTrak toll tag issued by FasTrak, or an axle-based toll will be charged.

Carpool

The following types of vehicles will be charged a discounted carpool toll of $2.50 in the designated carpool lane during the designated carpool hours:

  • Any 2-axle vehicle, without a trailer, carrying three or more persons
  • Any single-occupancy inherently-low-emission vehicle (ILEV) with DMV-issued decals
  • Any 2-axle vehicle, without a trailer, designated by the manufacturer to be occupied by no more than two person, carrying two persons
  • Any motorcycle

Note:

All carpool vehicles must use designated carpool lanes and use a FasTrak toll tag to pay for tolls.

Carpool hours are Monday through Friday between 5 a.m.-10 a.m. and 3 p.m.-7 p.m.

Pedestrians and Bicyclists

Pedestrians and bicyclists may cross the Antioch Bridge toll-free in designated lanes, in accordance with operational procedures.

Vehicle Definitions for Bridge Toll Assessment

Commute Bus: A Commute Bus shall include any vehicle that is:

  1. Designed, used, or maintained for the transportation of more than 10 persons including the driver;
  2. Operated across a state-owned toll bridge on a route commencing and terminating within a radius of 50 miles from the toll collection point of such bridge; and
  3. Operated on a regular schedule for any municipal or public corporation, transit district, public utility district or political subdivision, or private company for the nonprofit work-related transportation of its employees; or by any transportation company operating under a certificate of public convenience and necessity issued by the California Public Utilities Commission.

Vanpool Vehicle: A Vanpool Vehicle is a vehicle that is operating under the provisions of current ridesharing laws and regulations, and designed for carrying more than 10, but not more than 15, persons including the driver, that is maintained and used primarily for the nonprofit work-related transportation of persons for the purpose of ridesharing.

Motorcycle: Motorcycle shall include any motor vehicle having a seat or saddle for the use of the rider, with up to four wheels in contact with the roadway, two of which are a functional part of a sidecar.

Seating Capacity: If individual seats are provided, the number of such seats shall be used in determining the seating capacity. If individual seats are not used, seating capacity shall be determined on the basis of 17 inches of seat width per person.

Vehicle Combination: Vehicle Combination shall include any combination of motor-driven and drawn vehicle(s). Toll assessment will be based on the total number of axles on the roadway in the total combination.

Trailer: Trailer shall include any vehicle, including semi-trailer, designed for carrying persons or property and for being drawn by a motor vehicle.

Carpools

Buses, Carpools (THREE or more people) and Motorcycles with FasTrak toll tags receive discounted toll crossing ($4.25) on weekdays (except holidays) from 5 a.m. to 9 a.m. and 4 p.m. to 6 p.m. Eligible vehicles must have a valid FasTrak toll tag and an account in good standing to receive the discounted toll. Vehicles with dual tires are NOT eligible as carpools.

To receive the FasTrak discount toll during these hours you must have a valid FasTrak toll tag and travel in a designated carpool lane.

The Golden Gate Bridge carpool policy requires three or more people for two key reasons: to enhance traffic congestion and to remain within the budgetary requirements of GGBHTD.

Clean Air Vehicles

Clean Air Vehicles registered by the California Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) as a Clean Air Vehicle and displaying the official WHITE DMV decal (shown below) are allowed the FasTrak discounted carpool toll ($4.25) during carpool hours only, even if there are fewer than three people in the vehicle. That is, single- and double- occupant vehicles displaying the WHITE DMV decal qualify for the discounted toll rate during carpool hours provided they adhere to the carpool discount requirements stated above.

The 2012 DMV-issued GREEN decal DOES NOT qualify the vehicle for the discounted $4.25 carpool toll rate.

For official information about qualifying vehicles and DMV registration, call 800-242-4450.

NOTE: This special tag is required for Carpools and other HOVs to travel toll-free in the new I-580 Express Lanes in the Tri-Valley Area. This tag also functions as a regular FasTrak tag in all locations.

What You Need To Know

The I-580 Express Lanes are designated lanes in the Tri-Valley corridor - through Dublin, Pleasanton and Livermore. They are toll-free for carpools, vanpools, motorcycles, and eligible clean-air vehicles with the new FasTrak Flex tag displayed and set to Position (2) or (3+).

Solo drivers can use the Express Lane by paying a toll with a standard FasTrak tag or with the new FasTrak Flex tag set to Position (1).

When Do the Express Lanes Operate?

The I-580 Express Lanes operate during the same hours as HOV carpool lanes. Outside of these hours, the lanes are open to all vehicles at no charge.

How Much Do the Express Lanes Cost to Use?

Express lane tolls change depending on traffic conditions, increasing as traffic congestion increases and decreasing as traffic congestion decreases. The rate is displayed on overhead signs at the entrance to the Express Lanes.

The Express Lanes are free-of-charge to carpools and other qualified HOV vehicles.

Express Lanes improve the I-580 commute by spreading traffic flow more evenly across all lanes. Drivers have the choice to pay. Everyone benefits.

How To Use FasTrak Flex tags

Before you start your trip, set your FasTrak Flex tag to Position (1), (2), or (3+) to declare your toll status.

Solo Drivers:

Use Position (1) to pay your toll.

Carpoolers and other HOV-eligible vehicles:

On the I-580 Express Lanes you travel free with FasTrak Flex.

Use the (2) position if there are 2 people in the vehicle.

Use the (3+) position if there are 3 or more people in the vehicle.

Motorcycles and eligible clean air vehicles also use the (3+) position.

Note: To receive carpool discounts at toll bridges, you must use a designated carpool lane and have a valid FasTrak toll tag.

Want to start or join a carpool? Visit 511.org or call 511.

FasTrak Flex tags can be used at any toll facility displaying the FasTrak logo in California.

Express Lanes
I-680 South Express Lane

I-680 Southbound Express Lane

WHERE

The I-680 Southbound Express Lane is located on a 14-mile stretch of southbound I-680 from SR 84 to SR 237; 11 miles of the project is in Alameda County, with 3 miles in Santa Clara County.

PRICING

Toll costs vary based on real-time traffic levels in the Express Lane to ensure traffic in the lane continues to flow. This process is called dynamic pricing. During peak periods, when there is more traffic in the Express Lane, the toll is higher.

Tolls range from $.30 to $7.50.

HOURS OF OPERATION

The express lane operates Monday through Friday from 5 a.m. to 8 p.m. Nights and weekends, the lane is open to all drivers for free.

For more information: http://www.alamedactc.org/expresslanes

SR-237 Express Lanes

WHERE

Express lanes along the SR 237 and I-880 corridor in Santa Clara County are between just south of Dixon Landing Road on I-880 and North First Street on SR-237.

PRICING

Toll costs vary based on real-time traffic levels in the Express Lane to ensure traffic in the lane continues to flow. This process is called dynamic pricing. During peak periods, when there is more traffic in the Express Lane, the toll is higher.

Tolls range from $0.30 to $4.75, with an average toll of $1.60.

HOURS OF OPERATION

Express lanes will operate the same hours as carpool lanes, with the exception of an extended hour on westbound SR 237.

The express lane operates:

  • Monday through Friday from 5 a.m. to 10 a.m. Westbound
  • Monday through Friday from 5 a.m. to 9 a.m. Eastbound
  • Monday through Friday from 3 p.m. to 7 p.m. (both directions)

Midday, nights and weekends, the lane is open to all drivers for free.

Please note: At all times, however, drivers must adhere to the limited entry and exits and may not cross the double solid white lines. As stated above, the double white lines are in effect 24 hours a day, seven days a week, regardless of carpool or express lane hours of operation.

For more information: http://www.vta.org/projects-and-programs/highway/vta-express-lanes-sr-237-express-lanes-project

I-580 Express Lanes

I-580 Express Lanes

WHERE

The I-580 Express Lanes project spans approximately eleven miles from Hacienda Drive in Pleasanton to Greenville Road in Livermore in the eastbound direction, and approximately fourteen miles from Greenville Road to San Ramon Road/Foothill Road in Dublin in the westbound direction.

PRICING

Toll costs vary based on real-time traffic levels in the Express Lane to ensure traffic in the lane continues to flow. This process is called dynamic pricing. During peak periods, when there is more traffic in the Express Lane, the toll is higher.

Toll pricing is not available at this time.

HOURS OF OPERATION

The express lanes will operate the same hours as carpool lanes.

The express lane operates:

  • Monday through Friday from 5 a.m. to 9 a.m.
  • Monday through Friday from 4 p.m. to 7 p.m.

Midday, nights and weekends, the lane is open to all drivers for free.

For more information: http://www.alamedactc.org/expresslanes
