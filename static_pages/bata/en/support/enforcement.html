How is toll collection in express lanes enforced?

CHP currently enforces toll evasion and HOV occupancy on HOV lanes and express lanes. In the I-580 express lanes, and planned for other express lanes in the future, toll system cameras will capture images of vehicle license plates. If a valid FasTrak toll tag is not detected, an image of the vehicle license plate will be used to send a violation notice to the vehicle’s registered owner through the mail. The notice will include the toll amount plus a penalty for using the express lane without a valid FasTrak toll tag.

What happens if I use an express lane as a solo driver and without a FasTrak toll tag?

How much is the violation penalty for using an express lane without a valid FasTrak toll tag?

The first penalty for toll evasion is $25. If payment is not received by the due date, an additional $45 delinquent penalty is added. Additional penalties, including holds on the vehicle’s registration, third party collections, and garnishment of state tax refunds may be applicable. In addition, HOV violation penalties may apply.

What happens if a FasTrak Flex toll tag is switch to the 2 or 3+ position, but there is only one person in the vehicle?

CHP enforce HOV occupancy on express lanes. The FasTrak Flex toll tag will show CHP the vehicles that have declared themselves as eligible for toll-free travel by switching the tag to a toll-free setting. When a FasTrak Flex toll tag is set in the 2 or 3+ position, CHP is alerted that a vehicle is using the express lane toll-free and which toll exemption is being declared. CHP will visually confirm that these vehicles qualify for toll-free status; if not, CHP may pull the vehicle over. A carpool citation in excess of $400 may be issued to the driver.
