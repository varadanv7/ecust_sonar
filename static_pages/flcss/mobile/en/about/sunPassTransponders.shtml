TRANSPONDER TYPES AND DESCRIPTIONS

The most commonly used SunPass transponders are small, pocket sized devices that are attached to the inside of your car windshield, just below the rear view mirror. The transponder communicates via radio frequency with toll plaza readers and equipment. As the vehicle passes through SunPass equipped lanes, the toll charges are electronically deducted from your prepaid toll account.

There are two types of SunPass transponders:

SUNPASS MINI

PRICE $4.99

  • Must be permanently adhered to inside of windshield
  • Will NOT work if removed from windshield
  • Not for motorcycles
  • SunPass Plus Parking
SUNPASS PORTABLE

PRICE $19.99

  • Sticks to the inside of windshield with suction cups
  • Can carry it with you when you travel
  • Works on motorcycles
  • SunPass Plus Parking




SUNPASS INSTALLATION

As a SunPass customer, you are required to properly mount your transponder. Holding the transponder often causes inconsistent communications between the transponder and the lane reader.

Before installing, please see Special Windshield Considerations, below.

Installing SunPass Mini Transponder

Many SunPass customers utilize the SunPass Mini, even though it no longer works after it is removed from their vehicle’s windshield.

Installation Steps:

  1. Choose location from illustrations shown.
  2. Clean area using window cleaner.
  3. Peel off backing and apply.

Click here to see Mini Transponder Installation Instructions.

Installing SunPass Portable Transponder

The SunPass Portable Transponder is now easier than ever to install on the inside of your vehicle’s windshield due to the attached suction cups.

Installation Steps:

  1. See diagram for the placement of your transponder.
  2. Clean the area where you will be placing your transponder with rubbing alcohol or glass cleaner.
  3. Press your transponder against the windshield to enable the suction cups to stick. Transponder must be placed a minimum of 2 inches below the windshield frame. If the windshield has a solar strip, install the transponder at least 2 inches below the strip.
  4. Each suction cup has a “tab” that can be lifted to facilitate the removal of your portable transponder.


SPECIAL WINDSHIELD CONSIDERATIONS

The vehicles listed below may be equipped with special windshields containing metal oxide in the glass. This can prevent your SunPass transponder from being read. If you are unsure about whether your vehicle has one of these windshields, contact the dealer where you purchased your car. If your transponder is not always being read when driving through SunPass lanes, or if you need information on a Bumper Mount Transponder, please call the SunPass Customer Service Center at 1-888-TOLL-FLA (1-888-865-5352).
