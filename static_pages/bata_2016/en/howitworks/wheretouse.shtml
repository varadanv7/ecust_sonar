WHERE TO USE
You can use FasTrak to save time and money on all eight Bay Area toll bridges and in express lanes. Also you can use FasTrak to pay for parking at SFO. Learn how to take advantage of FasTrak to improve your driving experience at each of these Bay Area locations.

Tolls on the Antioch Bridge

Tolls are collected in the eastbound direction. Toll rates are based on the total number of axles on the roadway in a vehicle combination and are as follows (effective July 1, 2011):

Number of Axles Toll Rate   FasTrak
2 Axles $5.00   Passage for vehicles using FasTrak shall be at the same rates shown above, except for carpool vehicles as described below. For the location of FasTrak-only lanes on this bridge, click here.
3 Axles $15.00
4 Axles $20.00
5 Axles $25.00
6 Axles $30.00
7 or more Axles $35.00


Carpool

The following types of vehicles will be charged a discounted carpool toll of $2.50 in the designated carpool lane during the designated carpool hours:

  • Any 2-axle vehicle, without a trailer, carrying three or more persons
  • Any single-occupancy inherently-low-emission vehicle (ILEV) with DMV-issued decals
  • Any 2-axle vehicle, without a trailer, designated by the manufacturer to be occupied by no more than two person, carrying two persons
  • Any motorcycle
All carpool vehicles must use designated carpool lanes and use a FasTrak toll tag to pay for tolls.
Carpool hours are Monday through Friday between 5 a.m.-10 a.m. and 3 p.m.-7 p.m.

Commute Bus or Vanpool Vehicle

A Commute Bus or Vanpool Vehicle may cross toll-free at any time in designated lane(s), in accordance with operational procedures.

Passage through staffed lanes requires a toll-free commute bus ticket or a non-revenue FasTrak toll tag issued by FasTrak, or an axle-based toll will be charged.

Back to Top

Tolls on the Golden Gate Bridge

Tolls are collected on vehicles southbound into San Francisco only. There is no stopping to pay tolls on this bridge. You can use FasTrak in any lane. Toll rates are based on the total number of axles on the roadway in a vehicle combination and are as follows (effective July 1, 2018):

Number of Axles
2 Axles $8.00 $7.00
3 Axles $24.00 $21.00
4 Axles $32.00 $28.00
5 Axles $40.00 $35.00
6 Axles $48.00 $42.00
7 or more Axles $56.00 $49.00


Carpools

Buses, Carpools (THREE or more people) and Motorcycles with FasTrak toll tags receive discounted toll crossing ($5.00) on weekdays (except holidays) from 5 a.m. to 9 a.m. and 4 p.m. to 6 p.m. Eligible vehicles must have a valid FasTrak toll tag and an account in good standing to receive the discounted toll. Vehicles with dual tires are NOT eligible as carpools.

To receive the FasTrak discount toll during these hours you must have a valid FasTrak toll tag and travel in a designated carpool lane.

Clean Air Vehicles

Clean Air Vehicles registered by the California Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) as a Clean Air Vehicle and displaying the official WHITE DMV decal (shown below) are allowed the FasTrak discounted carpool toll ($5.00) during carpool hours only, even if there are fewer than three people in the vehicle. That is, single- and double- occupant vehicles displaying the WHITE DMV decal qualify for the discounted toll rate during carpool hours provided they adhere to the carpool discount requirements stated above.

The 2012 DMV-issued GREEN decal DOES NOT qualify the vehicle for the discounted $5.00 carpool toll rate.

For official information about qualifying vehicles and DMV registration, call 800-242-4450.

I-680 Sunol Express Lanes
Back to Top

(click to enlarge)

WHERE

The I-680 Sunol Express Lanes are located on a 14-mile stretch of southbound I-680 from SR-84 to SR-237; 11 miles of the project is in Alameda County, with 3 miles in Santa Clara County.

PRICING

Toll costs vary based on real-time traffic levels in the Express Lane to ensure traffic in the lane continues to flow. This process is called dynamic pricing. During peak periods, when there is more traffic in the Express Lane, the toll is higher.

Tolls range from $.50 to $7.50.

HOURS OF OPERATION

The express lanes operate Monday through Friday from 5 a.m. to 8 p.m. Nights and weekends, the lanes are open to all drivers for free.

For more information: http://www.alamedactc.org/expresslanes

SR-237 Express Lanes
Back to Top

(click to enlarge)

WHERE

Express lanes along the SR-237 and I-880 corridor in Santa Clara County are between just south of Dixon Landing Road on I-880 and North First Street on SR-237.

PRICING

Toll costs vary based on real-time traffic levels in the express lanes to ensure traffic in the lane continues to flow. This process is called dynamic pricing. During peak periods, when there is more traffic in the Express Lane, the toll is higher.

Tolls range from $0.30 to $4.75, with an average toll of $1.60.

HOURS OF OPERATION

The express lanes operate:

  • Monday through Friday from 5 a.m. to 10 a.m. Westbound
  • Monday through Friday from 5 a.m. to 9 a.m. Eastbound
  • Monday through Friday from 3 p.m. to 7 p.m. (both directions)

Midday, nights and weekends, the lane is open to all drivers for free.

Please note: At all times, however, drivers must adhere to the limited entry and exits and may not cross the double solid white lines. As stated above, the double white lines are in effect 24 hours a day, seven days a week, regardless of carpool or express lane hours of operation.

For more information: SR-237 Express Lanes Project

I-580 Express Lanes

WHERE

The I-580 Express Lanes project spans approximately eleven miles from Hacienda Drive in Pleasanton to Greenville Road in Livermore in the eastbound direction, and approximately fourteen miles from Greenville Roadd in Livermore to San Ramon Road/Foothill Road in Dublin in the westbound direction.

PRICING

Toll costs vary based on real-time traffic levels in the express lanes to ensure traffic in the lane continues to flow. This process is called dynamic pricing. During peak periods, when there is more traffic in the Express Lane, the toll is higher.

The toll pricing ranges from $0.50 to $13.00.

HOURS OF OPERATION

The express lanes operate Monday through Friday from 5 a.m. to 8 p.m.

Nights and weekends, the lanes are open to regular traffic for free.

For more information: http://www.alamedactc.org/expresslanes

I-680 Contra Costa Express Lanes
Back to Top

WHERE

The I-680 Contra Costa Express Lanes are located between Walnut Creek and San Ramon, spanning approximately twelve miles from Rudgear Road to Alcosta Boulevard in the southbound direction and approximately eleven miles from from Alcosta Boulevard to Livorna Road in the northbound direction.

PRICING

Toll costs vary based on real-time traffic levels in the express lanes to ensure traffic in the lane continues to flow. This process is called dynamic pricing. During peak periods, when there is more traffic in the Express Lane, the toll is higher.

The minimum toll pricing per zone is $0.50.

HOURS OF OPERATION

The express lanes operate Monday through Friday from 5 a.m. to 8 p.m.

Nights and weekends, the lanes are open to regular traffic for free.

For more information: I-680 in Contra Costa County
